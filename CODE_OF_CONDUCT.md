# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want BeardGit to be a welcoming, respectful, and productive project for everyone who participates, whether they are reporting a bug, proposing a feature, reviewing code, writing documentation, or helping other users.

We expect participants to act with empathy, professionalism, and care for the people behind the work.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that helps the community include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respecting different viewpoints, experience levels, and technical backgrounds.
- Giving constructive feedback that focuses on the work, not the person.
- Accepting responsibility, apologizing when appropriate, and learning from mistakes.
- Keeping discussions on topic and oriented toward improving BeardGit.
- Being patient with maintainers, contributors, and users.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of behavior that is not acceptable include:

- Harassment, intimidation, stalking, sustained disruption, or threats.
- Insults, personal attacks, trolling, or deliberately inflammatory comments.
- Public or private harassment based on personal characteristics or identity.
- Publishing someone else's private information without explicit permission.
- Sexualized language or imagery in project spaces.
- Dismissing or attacking people for asking questions in good faith.
- Any other conduct that the maintainers reasonably consider inappropriate in a professional open-source project.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all BeardGit project spaces, including the GitHub repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, release channels, and any public or private communication related to the project.

It also applies when someone represents the BeardGit project in public spaces.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it privately to the project maintainer at:

**adolfo_fuentes@outlook.com**

Please include as much relevant context as you can, such as links, screenshots, dates, usernames, and a clear description of what happened. Do not include sensitive personal information unless it is necessary for the report.

Reports will be handled with care and discretion. The maintainer will review the report, may ask for clarification, and will take action considered appropriate for the situation.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they consider necessary to protect the project and its participants, including:

- Asking for clarification or a change in tone.
- Editing, hiding, or deleting comments.
- Closing issues or pull requests.
- Temporarily or permanently restricting participation.
- Reporting serious abuse through the relevant platform channels.

People who are asked to stop unacceptable behavior are expected to comply promptly.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct follows the structure and intent of common open-source community standards, including the Contributor Covenant, while being adapted for BeardGit.
